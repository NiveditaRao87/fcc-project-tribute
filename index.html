Savitribai Phule

(1831-1897)


Introduction

Savitribai Phule was a Dalit woman and a pioneer of feminism in India. She fought against Brahmanical patriarchy. She was also the countryâ€™s first female teacher who went on to set up 17 more schools that imparted education to women of all castes. She worked alongside her husband and other women, to abolish caste and gender discrimination.

Time Line of Savitribai's life

  • 1831 Savitribai Jyotirao Phule is born at Naigon
  • 1840 is married to Jyotirao Phule at the age of 9
  • 1848 Starts India's first school for women along with Jyotirao Phule and Sagunabai in Bhide Wada
  • 1849 Along with Fatima Sheik starts a school for backward caste communities and Dalits
  • 1852 Starts the Mahila Seva Mandal and holds a strike against the practice of shaving of widow's heads
  • 1854 Publishes Kavya Phule, a book of poems
  • 1873 The SatyaShodhak Samaj(The Truth-Seeker's Society) is established
  • 1873 Holds first Satyashodhak marriage on 25 December
  • 1890 Jyotirao Phule passes away
  • 1897 Opens a clinic in Hadapsar for those affected by the bubonic plague
  • 1897 Dies from bubonic plague after caring for a patient
